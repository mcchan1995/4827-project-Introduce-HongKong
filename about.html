About Hong Kong

Hong Kong background

Hong Kong originally belonged to the territory of Guangdong in southern China. Before Hong Kong opened as a port, the cultural center in southern China was Guangzhou. Therefore, Hong Kong culture is in the same vein as Lingnan culture in Guangzhou and other places, for example, Cantonese is used as the main language. Before becoming a British colony, Weitou people, Hakka people and people from the water have all settled in Hong Kong today. Among them, people from the water are the descendants of the ancient Nanyue people who lived in the Pearl River Delta in ancient times. Among them, the Waitou and Shuiren dialects spoken by Waitou and Shuiren belong to the branch of Cantonese. After Hong Kong became a British colony, Western culture began to be introduced into Hong Kong. In the following hundred years, Chinese and Western cultures continued to develop in Hong Kong, and they influenced and merged with each other, gradually forming a Hong Kong culture combining Chinese and Western cultures. Many Chinese folk customs, such as the parade of colors, the Taiping Qing ceremonies, and the Yu Lan Festival, have developed uninterruptedly in the Chinese society. Herbal tea, Cantonese opera, and the culture of drinking morning tea are also very prosperous. Western cultures such as horse racing and football have gradually become popular in Chinese society. Hong Kong's food culture is also quite famous. A mix of Chinese dishes (such as Cantonese and Hakka dishes) and Western food, plus multi-national dishes can be found in Hong Kong, so it is known as a "food paradise". Among them, tea restaurants and big pai stalls are the representatives of authentic food culture.

Diet

Hong Kong is a place where Eastern and Western food cultures meet, so food culture also blends Chinese food (mainly Cantonese food and Hakka food) and Western food, plus multi-national dishes can be found in Hong Kong, so it is known as "food paradise" ". As a cosmopolitan city, food from all over the world is very common in Hong Kong. In the food culture of Hong Kong people, tea restaurants and big food stalls are the representatives of authentic food culture. Da Pai Stall is a stall that often provides open-air cooked food on the street. The MTR is one of the main means of transportation in Hong Kong, so its culture is closely related to Hong Kong society. Since the MTR and KCRC before the merger of the two railways already have a certain history, Hong Kong people will refer to them by the old names of the railway systems, routes and stations before the merger of the two railways, such as "Kowloon-Canton Railway" The MTR line operated by the railway, the current East Rail Line is called "(Electrified) train" or "East Rail", and the current Mong Kok East Station is called "Mong Kok Railway Station". In addition, MTR offers discounts to attract passengers to take the routes operated by MTR, and inherits the "MTR Club" established by the former MTR Corporation (the Chinese name was changed to "MTR Youlihui" after the merger of the two railways into MTR). ‚Äù, the English name remains unchanged). The stations of the MTR system have shops where passengers can buy food, newspapers and exchange coins at the stations. These shops have become a place for many passengers to meet others, making station shops one of the features of MTR stations. In addition, some railway fans will collect MTR tickets and MTR station guides, while netizens will adapt and imitate some MTR advertising posters. Newspaper distribution offices and customer service centers in MTR stations distribute different free newspapers respectively. In the early days, there were recruitment magazines such as Recruit and JobMarket. By the mid-2000s, free newspapers had emerged, and Metro Daily, Metro Shengshi, Toutiao Daily and am730 were distributed in the station. However, in recent years, the development of smart phones and online media has matured, and free newspapers and periodicals are no longer distributed.
